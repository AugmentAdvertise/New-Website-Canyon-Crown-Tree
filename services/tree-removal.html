Professional Tree Removal in Salt Lake City

There comes a time when a tree must be removed. Whether it is dead, diseased, structurally compromised, or simply in the wrong location, removing a tree safely requires the skill, experience, and equipment that only a professional arborist can provide. At Canyon Crown Tree, our ISA-certified arborists have been performing safe, efficient tree removals across Salt Lake City and Northern Utah since 2010.

When is tree removal necessary? Not every problem tree needs to come down. Our arborists always evaluate whether a tree can be saved through pruning, cabling, or health treatments before recommending removal. However, removal is the right choice when a tree is dead or dying and poses a falling hazard; when disease or decay has compromised the trunk or major structural roots; when storm damage has left the tree unstable; when roots are damaging foundations, driveways, or sewer lines; or when the tree simply needs to be cleared for construction or landscaping improvements.

Our removal process is methodical and safety-focused. Every job begins with a thorough on-site assessment by a certified arborist who evaluates the tree's size, species, condition, lean, proximity to structures, overhead utilities, and access constraints. From there, we develop a detailed removal plan that specifies the techniques, equipment, and crew needed to complete the job safely.

For straightforward removals, we use a combination of strategic limbing, sectional dismantling, and controlled felling. For complex removals near homes, garages, fences, or power lines, we deploy advanced rigging systems and, when necessary, crane assistance to lower sections precisely and safely. Our crews are trained in OSHA safety standards and carry $2 million in liability insurance, so your property and your peace of mind are fully protected.

Every removal includes complete cleanup. We chip all branches, remove all wood (or buck and stack firewood if you prefer), rake the area clean, and recycle 100% of the green waste. We leave your property looking better than when we arrived.

Our Tree Removal Process

Every tree removal follows a carefully planned process to ensure safety, efficiency, and complete satisfaction.

1

Assessment

A certified arborist visits your property to evaluate the tree's condition, size, species, lean direction, root structure, and proximity to structures and utilities. We identify any potential hazards and determine the best removal approach. You receive a detailed, written estimate with no hidden fees.

2

Planning

Based on the assessment, our team develops a removal plan specifying the technique (felling, sectional dismantling, or crane-assisted), equipment required, crew size, safety zones, and property protection measures. If permits are needed, we handle the process for you.

3

Execution

Our trained crew arrives on schedule with all necessary equipment. We establish safety perimeters, protect surrounding landscaping and structures, and systematically remove the tree according to plan. Every crew member follows OSHA safety protocols and ISA best practices throughout the operation.

4

Cleanup

Once the tree is down, we chip all brush, remove or stack wood as requested, rake the entire work area clean, and haul away all debris. If you want the stump ground as well, we can typically handle that the same day. We do not leave until you are 100% satisfied with the result.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about tree removal in Salt Lake City.

How much does tree removal cost in Salt Lake City?

Tree removal costs in Salt Lake City typically range from $300 to $3,000 or more, depending on the tree's size, location, condition, and accessibility. Small trees (under 30 feet) generally cost $300-$800, medium trees (30-60 feet) range from $800-$1,500, and large trees (over 60 feet) can cost $1,500-$3,000+. Crane-assisted removals for very large or difficult-access trees may be higher. We provide free, detailed estimates so you know exactly what to expect.

Do I need a permit to remove a tree in Salt Lake City?

In Salt Lake City, permits may be required for removing certain trees, particularly street trees or those in designated heritage areas. Private property trees generally do not require permits, but HOA rules may apply. Our team can help you determine whether a permit is needed and assist with the application process as part of our service.

How long does a tree removal take?

Most residential tree removals are completed in a single day, typically taking 2-8 hours depending on the tree's size, location, and complexity. Small, accessible trees may be done in 2-3 hours, while large removals requiring crane assistance or careful rigging near structures can take a full day. We always include complete debris cleanup in our timeline.

Is tree removal covered by homeowners insurance?

Homeowners insurance typically covers tree removal when a tree falls and damages a covered structure (your home, garage, fence, etc.) or blocks a driveway or accessibility ramp. Removal of standing dead or hazardous trees is usually not covered. We can help document damage for insurance claims and work directly with your adjuster to streamline the process.
