Complete Stump Removal for a Clean, Usable Yard

Old tree stumps are more than just an eyesore. They attract wood-boring insects like carpenter ants and termites, create tripping hazards for your family and visitors, obstruct mowing and landscaping, and can even send up persistent root suckers that crowd out surrounding plants. Canyon Crown Tree's professional stump grinding service eliminates these problems quickly and affordably.

Our process uses commercial-grade stump grinders equipped with carbide-tipped cutting teeth that mechanically chip away the stump and its major surface roots. Standard grinding takes the stump 6 inches below the soil surface—deep enough to lay sod, plant a garden bed, or simply achieve a level lawn. For homeowners who plan to replant a tree in the same location, install a patio, or pour a walkway, we offer deep grinding up to 12 inches below grade to ensure full clearance.

Stump grinding also addresses the visible surface roots that spread across lawns and garden beds. Our operators systematically grind exposed roots extending outward from the stump, giving you a smooth, even surface ready for landscaping. The remaining subsurface roots will decay naturally over time and pose no structural or pest concerns.

We call 811 before every job to mark underground utilities, and we carry full liability insurance. Your property is always protected.

After grinding is complete, you are left with a mound of wood chips mixed with soil. Most customers choose to backfill the hole with these chips and top off with fresh topsoil—it is the most economical option and the chips will decompose into rich organic matter over 12 to 24 months. Alternatively, we can haul all material away and deliver clean fill dirt. We also offer optional re-seeding and sod installation so the area blends seamlessly into your existing lawn.

How Stump Grinding Works

A simple, efficient process from estimate to a stump-free yard.

1

Free Estimate & Utility Check

We measure the stump, assess site access, and provide a flat-rate quote. We then call 811 to have underground utilities marked before any work begins.

2

Equipment Setup

Our crew positions the grinder and protects surrounding landscape features, structures, and irrigation lines. We select the right machine size for your site conditions.

3

Grinding Below Grade

The stump is ground 6 to 12 inches below the soil surface, along with any visible surface roots. Most stumps take 30 to 60 minutes to complete.

4

Backfill & Cleanup

We backfill the cavity with chips and topsoil, rake the area smooth, and clean up all debris. Optional re-seeding or sod installation is available on request.
